About


According to data from the World Health Organization (WHO), there are
more than 30 million animals abandoned in Brazil, of which 20 millions
are dogs.                                                                                                                      

According to recent data, the Federal District abandoned approximately
3,500 animals annually. Of that number, a large portion starves to death.

ASKAL, concerned with the death of animals due to hunger and with sus-
tainability, had the initiative to develop dispensers with Polythene Pipes
for feeding dogs. Knowing that the idea of ​​our project requires the support
of more people, dispensers will be placed in strategic places.

Our biggest differential will be the construction of a system that will warn
when the feed is running out, using a plate that will indicate how much the
food and water reach the minimum limit.

The board will automatically rise, displaying a help message for the people
passing by, and as soon as the dispenser is filled tissue again, the plate will
lower and people will become aware of the our project.
